Bone Age Assessments

Why do we need to know this?

Methods for measuring bone age

The commonly known "measuring bone age" refers to taking an X-ray of the left palm. Through this X-ray, a few trained doctors can estimate the overall age of the bones.

The principle of using bone age to assess growth

Doctors use X-rays of palm and finger bones to predict bone maturity based on their appearance, size, shape, and relationship with surrounding bones.

When should bone age be assessed?

When a child's growth and development is not as expected, such as being too tall or short, or developing too early or late, it is time to assess bone age

How often should bone age be assessed?

Testing bone age every six months is just right! Since the calculation of bone age relies heavily on the changes of hand bones over time, if the time between two X-rays is too close, there may not be enough changes. If the time is too far apart, there is a risk of unexpected changes in bone age.

How is bone age predicted?

To determine bone age, doctors examine palm and wrist X-rays for wrist bone development and size, palm and finger bone growth plate closure, and distal radius growth plate presence.
